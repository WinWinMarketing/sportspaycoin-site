A 24/7 market for tokenized sports cards, powered by Solana and a live performance oracle.

Executive Summary

Sports Pay Coin (SPC) takes high-value physical sports cards and turns them into liquid, fractional, on-chain assets. The exchange runs 24/7. A Sports Oracle prices each token to live game performance. Collectors get liquidity, fans get access, and dealers get a market they can move size on without discounting inventory.

This whitepaper covers the tokenization workflow, the Sports Oracle, the token economics, the presale structure, and the roadmap to a live exchange.

The Problem

📦

Illiquid Collectibles

High-value sports cards take weeks or months to sell. Auction cycles, escrow, shipping, insurance. Friction at every step. Capital is locked.

🚪

🎭

Counterfeit Risk

Fakes, "cleaned" graded cards, and swap scams plague the market. There is no immutable, public history of who owned what, and when.

📈

The Tokenization Workflow

Step 1: Asset Initiation & Vaulting

You ship your card to one of our partnered vault facilities. They handle authentication, climate-controlled storage, and full insurance from the moment it arrives. Vaults are established collectibles facilities, not something we built from scratch.

Step 2: Certification & Digital Minting

The vault partner authenticates the card, grades it if needed, and produces high-resolution imaging. Those records get minted on-chain as a digital twin: proof of ownership and provenance that can't be altered or faked.

Step 3: Liquidity & Exchange Listing

The token lists on the SPC exchange. Anyone can buy, sell, or trade it, whole or fractional, using SPC as the native currency. Settlement is instant. No more waiting weeks for an auction to close, or paying 10% to a house to do it.

The Sports Oracle

The Sports Oracle is the data feed that brings real-world sports information on-chain: final scores, player statistics, injury reports, social sentiment, and betting odds. Smart contracts read that feed and adjust token prices according to rules set in advance, with no human in the loop.

Without an oracle, a tokenized card behaves like a museum painting. Its price only moves when someone buys or sells. With an oracle, it behaves like a public stock that reacts to games, news, and sentiment in real time.

Oracle Capabilities

  • Fantasy-meets-investing: 5-touchdown game increases token value automatically
  • Real-time engagement: trade on injuries, buzzer-beaters, record performances
  • Automatic buy walls: smart contracts support prices during positive news
  • Social sentiment integration: Twitter buzz, fan polls, odds movement
  • Permissionless ecosystem: other developers can build on the oracle data
  • Reduced manipulation: rate-limited withdrawals and dispute mechanisms
  • Backtesting capability: historical data validates pricing models before launch
